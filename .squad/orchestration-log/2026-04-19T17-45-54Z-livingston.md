### Design Decisions

- **Progressive Density:** One-size-fits-all Grid → three view modes let users choose based on workflow
- **Grouping for Organization:** Mirrors real-world disc categorization (type, brand, bag) vs. flat list
- **Free-Form Tags:** Flexible + low-friction (vs. predefined list) for diverse user workflows
- **Filter Chips:** Visible, one-click toggles (vs. dropdown-buried filters) for discoverability
- **Column Sorting in List:** Spreadsheet pattern (click headers) more intuitive than dropdown for tabular layouts

## Learnings for Future Specs

- **Multi-view better than toggles** for density options — gives users true choice, not compromise
- **Mirror real-world workflows** in UX organization (grouping by type/brand/bag resonates with users)
- **Free-form data beats predefined lists** when workflows vary (tags more flexible than enums)
- **Make filters discoverable** — chips visible + one-click better than nested dropdowns
- **Design for scalability** — think about 30+ items from the start, not 5
- **Accessibility is design**, not QA — include ARIA/keyboard nav in spec, not as afterthought
